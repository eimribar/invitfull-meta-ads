Hey Tomer,

Quick update on what we did today with the Facebook ads.

**The problem**

The CBO campaign ($200/day, 17 creatives) had an algorithm laziness issue — Meta's Andromeda locked onto 3 ads and gave them 84% of the budget. The other 14 creatives barely got any spend. Our CPA climbed from $5 to $25 over the last week. The best performing ad ($5.08 CPA) was actually getting less budget than three worse performers.

**What we did**

1. Generated 88 new baby shower creatives using AI (Gemini), reviewed them, and approved 33.

2. Restructured the ad account based on research from 8 industry sources (Andromeda Blueprint V2, Foxwell Digital, VibeMyAd, etc.):
   - Paused the old CBO campaign
   - Created a new ABO testing campaign: 10 ad sets × $20/day × 2 creatives each = 20 ads, $200/day total
   - Each creative gets exactly $10/day — no more algorithm favoritism
   - 13 approved creatives held in reserve for the first refresh

3. Each ad has unique, tailored copy that matches its creative — no generic headlines.

**The new structure**

- **Campaign:** Baby Shower — ABO Testing — March 2026
- **Budget:** $200/day (same as before)
- **Ad sets:** 10, each with $20/day and 2 creatives
- **Creative mix:** Branded product ads, social proof (fake LinkedIn/Twitter posts), comparison ads, testimonials, before/after, UGC-style
- **Status:** Live as of today

**What happens next**

- **March 30 (Day 7):** First evaluation. We identify winners and losers.
- **Kill rule:** Any ad that spent $16+ with zero conversions gets killed.
- **Winner rule:** Any ad with 5+ conversions at $4 CPA or less is a winner.
- **After winners are found:** We launch a CBO scaling campaign with the winners (70% of budget) and keep ABO testing running (30%) to continuously find new winners.

**The goal**

Get CPA back down to $4 or below by finding creatives that actually work, instead of letting the algorithm pick favorites. The ABO structure guarantees every creative gets a fair shot with real budget.
